About Therese

Therese is a Registered Physiotherapist in Australia, She is a member of the Australian Physiotherapy Association and the Continence Foundation of Australia.

Therese has a degree in physiotherapy from the University of Cape Town and a Post Graduate Certificate in Pelvic Floor Physiotherapy from Melbourne University. Her initial interest in pelvic floor physiotherapy was sparked when she owned a physiotherapy practice in South Africa in the early 1990's. She has also lived and worked as a physiotherapist in New Zealand and currently lives and works in Australia.

She currently practices at Season's of Life Physiotherapy in the Northern suburbs of Melbourne, Australia. She also teaches safe Manual Handling to healthcare workers and is a consultant physiotherapist in 2 aged care facilities. In her spare time she moderates a LinkedIn Group, Worker's Compensation Australia, is slowly creating a Permaculture garden in her back yard and desperately trying to keep up with her two very busy teenage daughters and fitness mad husband.

As a mother she is determined that her two daughters will have a better understanding of how their bodies work than she did as a young woman.

When Therese was younger she was fanatical about swimming, running, windsurfing, gym classes and body building, but knew nothing about her pelvic floor. Due to her lack of knowledge about how her body was meant to function, she battled through her teens and twenties with mild stress and urge incontinence. Then using the techniques she learnt as a physiotherapist, she managed to regain control while she was expecting her first child.

These days Therese only leaks if she has forgotten her WeeFree exercises for a few weeks!
